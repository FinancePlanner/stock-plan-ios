# Monetization

## Recommended Model

Use a subscription model.

This product has ongoing value and ongoing costs:

- portfolio imports
- stock news
- earnings transcripts and summaries
- future voice features
- server-side processing and storage

Because of that, ads and a one-time purchase are a poor fit. The cleanest launch path is a free app with a paid auto-renewable subscription.

## Suggested Launch Structure

Start with:

- `Free`
- `Premium`

Avoid launching with too many tiers. Keep the paywall simple until usage data shows a clear reason to split plans further.

### Free

- 1 portfolio
- limited holdings
- manual expense planning
- basic or delayed news
- 1 saved valuation case
- limited stock comparisons

### Premium

- portfolio import
- unlimited holdings and watchlists
- full expense planner and reports
- bear, base, and bull cases
- fair value tracking
- peer comparison
- multi-year projections
- earnings text
- social sharing/export

### Premium + AI Later

Add this only if voice and AI features create real marginal cost:

- voice earnings
- AI summaries
- more alerts
- expanded saved cases or exports

## Pricing to Test First

Initial pricing to test:

- `€11.99/month`
- `€89.99/year` to `€99.99/year`
- 7-day or 14-day free trial on annual

The annual plan should be the default highlighted option on the paywall.

## Why This Fits The Product

The app combines two jobs:

- personal financial planning
- stock research and portfolio decision support

That gives it higher perceived value than a single-purpose budgeting app, but it still needs to earn trust before moving upmarket on price. A yearly plan around `€89–119` is a reasonable starting range to validate willingness to pay.

## Product Positioning

Recommended positioning:

- `Your personal finance operating system.`
- `Plan your money. Research your stocks. Keep your decisions in one place.`

This keeps the product framed as planning and research support, not financial advice.

## What To Avoid

- ads
- affiliate-heavy broker pushing at launch
- separate paid plans for `Expenses` and `Stocks` at MVP
- claiming stock picks or investment advice

Finance products need trust. Ads and overly aggressive monetization weaken that immediately.

## Recommended App Store Setup

Use one subscription group with:

- one monthly product
- one annual product

Keep the app free to download. Gate premium workflows inside the app.

## Feature Gating Recommendation

Keep these premium at launch:

- portfolio import
- advanced stock research features
- full reports
- unlimited saved valuation scenarios
- earnings text and future voice features

Keep these free to drive activation:

- basic manual portfolio creation
- a lightweight expense planner
- a limited stock comparison experience

## Future Monetization Options

Later, if usage justifies it:

- add a higher AI tier
- add consumable credits for heavy voice usage
- add team/family plans if collaborative use appears

Do not add complexity until retention and conversion data justify it.
